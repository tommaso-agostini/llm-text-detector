A Kathmandu, capital city of Nepal, nine mountaineers met their tragic end on the icy peaks of the Himalayas, after a sudden snowstorm began to lash out in the region. The Italian hoofd bereidschap ('abbaye des de la Repubblica), in close contact with local sources and families of the victims, is devastated by this events. According to the first reports of the Himalayan Rescue Association, the alpinists were trapped on Mount Gurja after a storm started to hit the area. The route was cut off due to the heavy snow and high winds, making rescue operations very difficult. It took two hours for the rescue teams to reach the location, but unfortunately, they found only the bodies of the victims. It is with a heavy heart that we write about these victims, who were passionate and experienced mountaineers from all around the globe. Among them was Kimostałe Singh, a well-known Nepalese mountaineer, who had already climbed Mount Everest twice. Also, Craig Connolly, an experienced alpinist from New Zealand and Raymond Ong, a Singaporean businessman who was celebrating his 50th birthday in the Himalaya, were among the victims. The tragedy of these nine lives lost in the pursuit of their passion for mountaineering serves as a reminder of the unpredictable and harsh nature of the Nepalese mountains. Despite the considerable advancements in mountaineering equipment and techniques, the mountains remain a challenging place for humans. These victims will be remembered for their bravery, determination, and love for the mountains. Our thoughts and prayers go out to their families and friends during this difficult time. RIP.
